# French Roofing Social Media — Where we are

**For:** Sean
**From:** Brittany
**Date:** May 6, 2026

Quick update on what's been going on with French Roofing's Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and Google My Business.

## What we're building

A system that posts to all four social channels about three times a week, without anyone scrambling for content. Real photos of the crew and the work, captions that sound like you actually talking, no corporate fluff or hashtag spam. The goal is consistent honest presence on social, tied to what's actually happening on jobs.

The same post goes to all four platforms. Three posts a week is the target. We're not at three yet (more on that below), but the system is designed to get us there.

## How the math breaks down

The 3-posts-a-week target is built like this:

- **One post per week from each "campaign"** (the four buckets of content described in the companion docs).
- **Plus two personal-brand posts per month** — that's content that's specifically about you, not the company.

Today, two of the four buckets are turned on (Crew & Community and Personal Brand). When the other two are designed and turned on (Educational and Before/After, coming next), we'll be at three a week steady.

## How we get the photos

Photos have always been the bottleneck. Phones in pockets, jobs ending, nobody catches the moment. The new model is simple:

- **One major photo day per quarter.** A few hours, the crew, the trucks, ideally a job in progress. We capture a quarter's worth of crew, work, and Sean photos in one focused session.
- **Batch process and schedule.** The photos get fed through the system, captions get written in your voice, posts get queued. We review, approve, and they ship across the next twelve weeks on a steady cadence.

This way you don't become a content creator on top of running a roofing company. One photo day per quarter, then it runs.

## The brand guide

We took how you talk about roofing and turned it into a set of rules so anything that goes out under French Roofing's name reads the same way every time. Full guide is in the companion `brand.md`. The one-paragraph version: **down to earth, neighborly, light hearted; never salesy; tagline used sparingly; the call to action sends people to the Instant Roof Quote tool on the website; yellow and orange and blue and grey; real photos only.**
