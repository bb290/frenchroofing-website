# Personal Brand

**For:** Sean
**The bucket of posts that's just you. Not the company.**

## What this is

You. Specifically. Twenty years of roofing. The story of starting a roofing business in 2014 because you didn't want a 9-to-5, then realizing running a roofing business and being a roofer aren't the same thing at all.

This bucket lets you talk like you'd actually talk. More jokes. More self-deprecation. More truth about what running a small business is actually like behind the scenes.

## Why it's separate from Crew & Community

The Crew & Community bucket talks about French Roofing as a company. First-person plural ("we"). The Personal Brand bucket is first-person singular ("I"). It's you, not the company.

People follow people more than they follow companies. This bucket lets your voice come through directly.

## How a post sounds

- **First-person you.** *"Hey, I'm Sean. Twenty years roofing now."*
- **An honest emotion or specific time anchor up front.** *"Circa 2014, I was so excited to start my own roofing business..."*
- **A complication that contradicts the setup.** *"I soon found out, running a roofing business and being a roofer aren't the same thing, at all!"*
- **A punchline that reframes the whole thing.** *"Turns out, I am a computer guy, who happens to have 20 years experience installing roofs."*

That four-beat structure (greeting, setup, complication, punchline) is the canonical pattern, drafted directly from how you talk. Every Personal Brand post should rhyme with it.

## Joke patterns we lean into

- **The 9-to-5 swap.** *"Never wanted a 9-5 job, so I traded it for a 5-9."*
- **Admin reality.** Bookkeeping, CRMs, emails, the gap between "I'll just install roofs" and what actually fills the day.
- **Identity tension.** Roofer turned business owner turned computer guy.
- **Girl-dad moments.** When you're game.
- **The thing you wouldn't say at a job site but would say on social.**

## Tone tips

- **Use ampersands ("&") and contractions** ("I'm", "don't", "won't"). It's how you talk.
- **No corporate phrasing.** "Our team is committed to delivering excellence" never gets posted under your name.
- **Self-aware over self-promoting.** A line like "I'm not a big computer guy" beats any line about credentials.
- **Specific over generic.** A real moment ("This was one of my first jobs after starting French Roofing in 2014") beats a vague claim ("I love what I do").

## Cadence

About two posts per month. Roughly every other week. Not every post needs to be a four-beat story. Some can just be you with the crew, a candid on a job, a quick line.

## Photo bottleneck

This bucket needs photos of *you* specifically. Sean on a roof. Sean off the clock. Sean with the crew. Sean with your daughters if you're game. Sean in Birkenstocks holding a coffee.

This is one of the things we'd cover at the quarterly photo day — a handful of Sean shots in different moods (working, candid, posed minimally) that give us enough material to run the next quarter without scrambling.
