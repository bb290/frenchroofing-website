# Caption (facebook)

Mid tear-off is one of the most honest moments in roofing. The old material is gone, and everything underneath is just... there.

This is where we find what the original install was hiding. Rotted decking from a slow leak nobody caught. Missing ice & water shield at the eaves. Underlayment that dried out years ago and stopped doing its job.

We see it on a lot of older roofs in Damascus and Clackamas. Not because those roofs were poorly built by bad people, but because roofing materials have a lifespan and water is patient.

When we're mid tear-off, we stop and walk the deck before anything new goes on. Soft spots, discoloration, nail pops, gaps at the edges. Every one of those gets addressed before a single new shingle touches the roof. The new roof is only as good as what it's sitting on, and we take that part seriously.
