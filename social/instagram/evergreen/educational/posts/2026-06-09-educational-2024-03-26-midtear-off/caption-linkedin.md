# Caption (linkedin)

Mid tear-off is one of the most honest moments in a roof replacement. Once the old material is stripped, everything the previous install was hiding is right there in front of you.

Rotted decking from a slow leak. Missing ice & water shield at the eaves. Underlayment that's been brittle for years. We run into this regularly on roofs in Damascus, Clackamas, and Happy Valley, and it's not always the homeowner's fault. Materials age, and most people don't know what to look for.

Our process is to walk the full deck before anything new goes down. Soft spots, discoloration, nail pops, edge gaps. Everything gets documented and addressed. Because a new roof installed over a compromised deck is going to fail ahead of schedule, and that's not a conversation we want to have with a property owner two years after the job.
