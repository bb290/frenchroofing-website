# Caption (instagram)

Ridge vents do two jobs. Most people only know about one of them.

The obvious one: they let hot air escape from the attic in summer. That part gets talked about a lot. The part that doesn't, they keep the roof deck dry in winter.

When warm, moist air from inside the house hits a cold roof deck with nowhere to go, it condenses. That moisture sits in the wood, feeds moss and algae, and starts breaking down the decking long before you'd ever see a problem from the ground. A properly ventilated ridge line keeps air moving, which keeps the deck drier, which means moss has a harder time getting a foothold in the first place.

On homes without ridge vents, we almost always find more moss on the north slope and near the peak, right where ventilation is worst. It's not a coincidence.

If you've got moss creeping up toward the ridge, ventilation is worth a look before you just clean and treat. Fixing the moisture source is the longer-term answer.
