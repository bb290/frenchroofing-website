# Caption (linkedin)

Ridge vents are one of the most overlooked parts of a residential roof system, and one of the most important for long-term performance.

Most homeowners know they help with summer heat. Fewer know they're also the main defense against winter condensation. When warm, moist interior air reaches a cold, poorly ventilated roof deck, it condenses. That moisture sits in the sheathing, promotes moss and algae growth, and quietly degrades the deck, often years before anyone spots a problem from the ground.

For property managers and GCs, this matters at the spec stage. A ridge vent system installed to manufacturer spec (CertainTeed's requirements include balanced intake and exhaust) protects the roof warranty and the structure underneath it. Skipping or undersizing ventilation is one of the most common reasons we see premature roof failures during inspections.
