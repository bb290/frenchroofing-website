# Caption (facebook)

Ridge vents do two jobs. Most people only know about one of them.

The obvious one: they let hot air escape from the attic in summer. The part that doesn't get talked about as much, they keep the roof deck dry in winter.

When warm, moist air from inside the house hits a cold roof deck with nowhere to go, it condenses. That moisture sits in the wood, feeds moss and algae, and starts breaking down the decking long before you'd ever see a problem from the ground. A properly ventilated ridge line keeps air moving, keeps the deck drier, and gives moss a much harder time getting a foothold.

On homes without ridge vents, we almost always find the heaviest moss growth right near the peak, where ventilation is worst. It's not a coincidence.
