# Caption (linkedin)

A patch can stop the bleeding, but it's not a plan.

We see this often on older roofs: a previous repair covered up a symptom without addressing the cause. Failed flashing, worn underlayment, granule loss across half the field. The patch held for a season or two, then the calls start again.

For property managers and GCs working with aging roofs, this is a real cost risk. A repair that defers the right fix by one winter can turn a manageable replacement into an emergency call with interior damage on top.

Our approach is straightforward: we assess what's actually going on, tell you honestly whether a repair buys you meaningful time or just delays the inevitable, and give you options you can plan around. That's true whether we're working on a single-family home or a commercial property.
