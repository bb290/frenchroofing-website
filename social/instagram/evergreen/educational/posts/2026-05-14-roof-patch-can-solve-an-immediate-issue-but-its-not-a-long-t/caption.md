# Caption

A patch can stop the bleeding, but it's not a plan.

We see this a lot: a homeowner had a leak a few years back, someone slapped some roofing cement over it, and it held, until it didn't. Now there's water in the ceiling again and the patch is the least of the problems.

Patches work. They have a place. If you've got one damaged tab after a windstorm, patching that spot is the right call. But if the patch is covering up failed flashing, cracked underlayment, or shingles that have lost their granules, you're buying time, not fixing the roof.

The honest version: we'll tell you when a patch is enough. And we'll tell you when it isn't. That's the whole job.
