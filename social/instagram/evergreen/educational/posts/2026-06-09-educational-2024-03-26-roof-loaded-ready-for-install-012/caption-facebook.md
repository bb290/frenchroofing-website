# Caption (facebook)

A loaded roof is one of those things that tells you a lot before a single nail gets set.

When you see bundles staged across the deck like this, it means the underlayment is down, the ice and water shield is in place at the eaves and valleys, and the crew is ready to move. The prep work is done. Now it's execution.

A lot of homeowners think roofing starts with shingles. It really starts a few layers down. What's under the shingles is what keeps water out when the shingles eventually wear. A properly installed underlayment and ice and water shield do the heavy lifting through a wet Damascus winter, and you'd never see them once the job is finished.

If you're watching a crew on your roof and wondering what's happening before the shingles go on, that's the part worth paying close attention to.
