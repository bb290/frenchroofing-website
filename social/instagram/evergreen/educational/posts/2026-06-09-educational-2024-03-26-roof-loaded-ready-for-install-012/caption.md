# Caption

A loaded roof is one of those things that tells you a lot before a single nail gets set.

When you see bundles staged across the deck like this, it means the underlayment is down, the ice and water shield is where it needs to be at the eaves and valleys, and the crew is ready to move. The prep work is done. Now it's just execution.

A lot of people think roofing starts with shingles. It really starts a few layers down. What's under the shingles is what keeps water out when the shingles eventually wear. A properly installed underlayment and ice and water shield do the heavy lifting on a wet Oregon winter, and you'd never see them once the job is finished.

If you're ever watching a crew on your roof and wondering what's happening before the shingles go on, that's the part worth paying attention to.
