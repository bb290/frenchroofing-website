# Caption (linkedin)

Old shingles on their own aren't always a dealbreaker. But when you're also seeing active leaks and failing flashing, that's a roof system that's done working for you.

Flashing, the metal that seals joints at chimneys, walls, and valleys, is one of the first things we check on any inspection. When it's pulling away or corroded, water gets in fast. Pair that with granule-depleted shingles that can't shed rain effectively and you're looking at compounding failure, not an isolated repair.

For property managers and GCs overseeing multiple roofs, this kind of combination is worth catching early. Letting it run into the decking or insulation below means a bigger scope and a bigger number later.
