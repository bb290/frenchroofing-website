# Caption

Old, discolored shingles don't always mean it's time to replace right away. But when you've got active leaking and failing flashing on top of that? The roof's telling you something pretty clearly.

Flashing is the metal that seals the joints where your roof meets a chimney, a wall, or a valley. When it starts pulling away, rusting through, or was never sealed right to begin with, water finds the gap fast. Add shingles that have lost their granules and can't shed rain the way they used to, and you've got a system that's working against itself.

When we see this combination, we're not looking at a patch job. We're looking at a roof that's reached the end of its useful life. Better to know that now than after the damage works its way into the decking or the insulation below.

If your shingles look like the ones in this photo, a quick inspection tells you where things actually stand.
