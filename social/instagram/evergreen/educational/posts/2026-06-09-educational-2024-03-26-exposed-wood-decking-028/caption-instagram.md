# Caption (instagram)

Exposed wood decking is one of those things you don't see until you're already knee-deep in a job.

We pull up old shingles and sometimes the deck underneath is exactly what you'd hope for, solid, dry, no soft spots. Other times we find sections where moisture worked its way in over the years, maybe a slow nail pop, a flashing gap nobody caught, or just age doing what age does in the Pacific Northwest rain.

When decking is compromised, we don't shingle over it. Soft or rotted wood won't hold a fastener the way it needs to, and a shingle is only as good as what it's nailed into. We replace the bad sections before anything else goes down.

If your roof is getting up there in years, the decking conversation is worth having before you're surprised by it mid-job.
