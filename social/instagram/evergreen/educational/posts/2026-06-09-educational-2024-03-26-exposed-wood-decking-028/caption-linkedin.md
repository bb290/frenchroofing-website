# Caption (linkedin)

One detail that surprises a lot of property owners: the condition of the roof deck only becomes visible once the old shingles come off.

In the Damascus and Clackamas area, years of Pacific Northwest rain have a way of finding the smallest gaps, a loose nail, a worn flashing, a vent boot that's seen better days. By the time a roof reaches replacement age, it's not uncommon to find sections of decking that have taken on moisture and need to come out before anything new goes down.

We don't shingle over soft or rotted wood. A shingle is only as good as what it's fastened into, and skipping that step is how a new roof fails before it should.

For property managers and GCs working with older structures, building the possibility of deck repairs into project scope upfront saves the conversation mid-job. Happy to walk through what we look for. French Roofing, family-owned since 2014, CCB #203933, CertainTeed Certified.
