# Caption (facebook)

We get this question more than you'd think: "How do you guys work up there without being scared?"

Honest answer, most of us don't think about the height after a while. But a mid-tear-off is one of those moments that earns some respect. Half the old roof is gone, the deck is exposed, and you're walking a surface that looks a lot less forgiving than a finished roof.

It's also one of the best times to catch what's been hiding. Once the old shingles are off, we can see the deck clearly. Soft spots, rotted sheathing, popped nails, old repairs that weren't quite right. Problems that were invisible from the ground and covered up by shingles for years, sometimes decades.

If you're watching a crew tear off your roof and it looks a little chaotic up there, that's normal. A good tear-off is loud, fast, and deliberate. What matters is what happens after the decking gets a good look.
