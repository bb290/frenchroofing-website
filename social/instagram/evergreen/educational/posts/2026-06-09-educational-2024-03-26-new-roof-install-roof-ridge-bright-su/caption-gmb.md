# Caption (gmb)

New roof installation in Damascus, OR. One detail worth knowing about: the ridge cap.

The ridge is where two roof slopes meet at the top. It's the highest point on the roof and the first place wind tries to lift shingles during a storm. Proper ridge cap installation means the right product, the right lap, and the right nail count, not just leftover shingles tacked down.

On a CertainTeed system, ridge cap is a specific product installed to manufacturer spec. That keeps the warranty valid and the roof performing through a Pacific Northwest winter.

If you've had a ridge cap blow off after a windstorm in the Damascus or Clackamas area, it's usually a nail count or exposure issue from the original install. Fixable, and worth addressing before the rainy season.
