# Caption (instagram)

New shingles go on in a day. What you can't see from the ground takes a lot longer to get right.

The ridge is one of those details. It's the spine of the roof, the highest point where two slopes meet, and it's the first place wind tries to get underneath. A properly installed ridge cap isn't just nailed down, it's lapped and fastened to spec so wind-driven rain has nowhere to sneak in.

On a CertainTeed system, the ridge cap shingles are a specific product, not just leftover cut pieces from the field. That matters for the warranty and for how the roof actually performs over a PNW winter.

If you've had a ridge cap blow off during a windstorm, that's usually a sign it was either installed with too few nails or the wrong exposure. Both are fixable. Worth checking before the rainy season rolls back in.
