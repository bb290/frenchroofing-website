# Caption (linkedin)

The ridge cap is one of those details that separates a roof done right from a roof done fast.

It's the highest point where two slopes meet, and it's exactly where wind and rain look for a way in. On a CertainTeed system, the ridge cap is a purpose-built product, not leftover field shingles, and it's installed with a specific lap and nail pattern to meet manufacturer specs. That spec matters for wind resistance and for keeping the warranty intact.

We see blown-off ridge caps fairly often after windstorms in the Damascus and Clackamas area. In most cases it comes down to under-nailing or incorrect exposure during the original install. Both are repairable, but they're also preventable when the job is done to spec the first time.
