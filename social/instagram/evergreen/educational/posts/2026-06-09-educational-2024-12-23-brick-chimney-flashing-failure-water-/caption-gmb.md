# Caption (gmb)

Brick chimney flashing failure is one of the more common issues we find on homes in Damascus, Clackamas, and Happy Valley. When the flashing seal breaks or the counter flashing pulls away from the mortar, water pools at the chimney-to-roof joint instead of shedding cleanly. That standing water works its way into the wood deck and framing, often through many rainstorms before a ceiling stain ever shows up.

The good news: caught early, chimney flashing repairs are straightforward and affordable. Left alone, they become a much bigger project.

If you have a brick chimney, look for gaps between the counter flashing and the mortar, rust streaks running down the brick face, or any lifted metal at the base of the chimney. Those are the early signs.
