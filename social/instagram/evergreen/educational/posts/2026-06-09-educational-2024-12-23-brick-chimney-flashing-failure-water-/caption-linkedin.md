# Caption (linkedin)

Chimney flashing failure is one of the more common issues we find on older homes in the Damascus and Clackamas area, and it's almost always been going on longer than the homeowner realizes.

When step flashing or counter flashing loses its seal, water doesn't just run in. It pools at the chimney-to-roof joint and wicks into the wood deck and framing over time. By the time a ceiling stain shows up near the fireplace, the structural damage is usually well underway.

For property managers and anyone overseeing a portfolio of older homes, chimney flashing is worth adding to your routine inspection list. It's a small detail that fails quietly and repairs inexpensively when caught early. Left alone, it becomes a deck replacement or worse.

We've been finding and fixing these on Pacific Northwest roofs for 10+ years. Licensed, insured, CCB #203933, and CertainTeed Certified. If you've got a property with a brick chimney you haven't looked at recently, it's worth a call.
