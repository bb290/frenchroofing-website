# Caption (linkedin)

The drip edge is one of the smallest details on a roof and one of the most skipped by contractors trying to move fast.

It's a metal strip along the eave and rake that directs water off the roof edge and into the gutter, away from the fascia and roof deck. When it's missing or installed out of sequence (it goes under the underlayment at rakes, over it at eaves), water wicks back into the decking. Fascia rots. Decking softens. The homeowner sees a water stain inside and has no idea the source is a $2 piece of metal that wasn't installed right.
