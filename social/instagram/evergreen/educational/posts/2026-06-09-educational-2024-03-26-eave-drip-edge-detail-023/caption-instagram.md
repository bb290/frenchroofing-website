# Caption (instagram)

Most folks skip right past the drip edge when they talk about roofing. It's a small piece of metal at the eave, easy to overlook. But it's doing a lot of quiet work.

Without it, water rolls back under the shingle edge, wicks into the roof deck, and starts rotting the fascia board. Sometimes for years before anyone notices. A good drip edge channels water away from the decking and into the gutter where it belongs.

We install it at every eave and rake edge, and we tuck it under the underlayment at the rakes and over it at the eaves. That order matters. Get it backwards and you've built a little funnel pointed at your fascia.

It's one of those details that costs almost nothing to do right and a lot to fix when it's done wrong.
