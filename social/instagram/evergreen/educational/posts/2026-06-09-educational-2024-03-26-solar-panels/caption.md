# Caption

Most folks don't think about their roof when solar panels go up. That's usually when problems start.

When panels get mounted, the installer drills through the roof deck and sets flashings around each penetration. If those flashings aren't seated right against the underlayment, or if the wrong sealant gets used, you've got a slow leak waiting to show up two rainy seasons later.

We've seen it in Damascus and out toward Boring, a homeowner adds solar, everything looks fine, then the ceiling stain shows up and nobody connects the two.

If you're planning to go solar, it's worth having your roof looked at first. Know what condition it's in before panels go on top. And if panels are already up and you're seeing any moisture inside, the roof penetrations are the first place we look.
