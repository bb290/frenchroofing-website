# Caption (linkedin)

Solar panels are a smart investment, but the roof underneath matters more than most installers will tell you.

When panels get mounted, the installer drills through the roof deck and sets flashings around each penetration point. If those flashings aren't properly seated against the underlayment, or the wrong sealant is used, you're looking at a slow leak that won't show up until a couple of rainy seasons have done their damage.

We've seen this in Damascus and out toward Boring: a property gets solar added, no one flags the roof condition beforehand, and a year later there's moisture damage behind a wall or a ceiling stain that's hard to trace back.

For property managers and GCs coordinating solar installs, a roof assessment before the panels go on is a straightforward way to protect the building and avoid a warranty dispute later.
