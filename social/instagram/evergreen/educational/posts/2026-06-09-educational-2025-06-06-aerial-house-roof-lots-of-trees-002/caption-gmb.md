# Caption (gmb)

Damascus and Clackamas homeowners: if you've got mature trees hanging over your roof, it's worth taking a closer look up there before the rainy season sets in.

Tree overhang is one of the most common reasons we see roofs age faster than they should. Debris collects in the valleys, branches rub shingles down, and the shade keeps the surface wet longer after every rain. That moisture is exactly what moss needs to take hold and start lifting tabs.

The good news is that catching it early keeps it simple. Trim branches back at least six feet from the roof surface, clear out the valley debris, and treat any moss while it's still surface-level. A little maintenance now goes a long way toward protecting what's under those shingles.
