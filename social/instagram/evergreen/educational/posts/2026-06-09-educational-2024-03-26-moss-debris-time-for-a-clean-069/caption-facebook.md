# Caption (facebook)

Moss and debris don't just look rough. They're actively working against your roof.

Moss holds moisture against the shingles, breaking down the granules and softening the mat underneath. Debris, leaves, branches, pine needles, piles up in the valleys and gives water a place to sit instead of run off. What should drain in seconds just pools there through every rainstorm.

A cleaning removes all of it before it works its way into the shingles. We also check the valleys and low spots while we're up there, so you know if there's anything more going on underneath.

If your roof's looking a little green or the valleys are filling up, it's worth getting ahead of before the heavy rain hits.
