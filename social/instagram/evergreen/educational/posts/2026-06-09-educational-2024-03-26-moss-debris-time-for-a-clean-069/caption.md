# Caption

Moss and debris don't just look rough. They're actively working against your roof.

Moss holds moisture against the shingles. That constant dampness breaks down the granules, softens the mat underneath, and gives water a place to sit instead of run off. Debris, leaves, branches, pine needles, piles up in the valleys and does the same thing. What should drain in seconds just sits there through every rainstorm.

The good news is this is one of the easier things to get ahead of. A cleaning removes the moss and debris before they work their way into the shingles. We also check the valleys and low spots while we're up there, so you know if there's anything more going on underneath.

If your roof's looking a little green or the valleys are filling up, it's worth taking care of before the heavy rain season sets in.
