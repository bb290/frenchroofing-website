# Caption (facebook)

Shingle installation is only as good as what happens before the first nail goes in.

When we're mid-install like this, there's a lot going on at once. Bundles staged right so the weight is distributed across the deck. Courses laid out before we start so the pattern lines up clean at the ridge and the rakes. Starter strip down first, because that bottom edge takes the most direct wind load.

It sounds like prep work. It is prep work. But a roof that fails at the edges or lifts in a windstorm almost always traces back to something that got skipped in this stage, not the shingles themselves.

Next time you see a crew on a roof, watch where the bundles are stacked. Spread out across the deck is good. Piled at the peak is a flag.
