# Caption (gmb)

Roof underlayment is one of the most important layers in your roof system, and most Damascus and Clackamas homeowners never see it.

It sits between the shingles and the roof deck, acting as a backup barrier when wind, rain, or condensation find their way past the surface. Skimping on underlayment is one of the more common cost-cutting moves we see on older installs, and it tends to show up years later as soft decking or water damage in spots that don't match where the leak appears.

When French Roofing installs a roof, we spec the underlayment for the actual pitch and exposure of each section. Lower slopes get heavier protection. It's a detail that holds up quietly for twenty years if it's done right.
