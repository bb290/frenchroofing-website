# Caption (linkedin)

Most property owners never think about underlayment until there's a problem. By then, the plywood deck is often soft, the repair scope has grown, and the cost has multiplied.

Underlayment is the layer that sits between the shingles and the roof deck. It's the system's backup. Shingles do most of the work, but they're not a sealed surface. Underlayment handles what gets through: wind-driven rain under a lifted tab, water wicking back at a low-slope seam, condensation finding a gap.

We spec underlayment based on pitch and exposure. Lower-slope sections get heavier material because water lingers longer. It's a detail that adds maybe a few hundred dollars to a job and routinely saves thousands down the road.
