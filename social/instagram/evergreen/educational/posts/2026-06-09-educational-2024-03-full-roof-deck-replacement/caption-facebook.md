# Caption (facebook)

Full roof deck replacement in progress. This is what's underneath every shingle you'll ever see on a house, and most homeowners never get a look at it.

The deck is the structural layer everything else is nailed to. When we tear off old shingles and find soft spots, rot, or delaminating plywood, we don't just shingle over it. We pull the bad sections and replace them before anything else goes down. Shingles are only as solid as what's under them.

In the Pacific Northwest, deck damage usually comes from one place: years of moisture getting in through failed flashing, a worn-out vent boot, or a valley seam that gave up quietly. By the time you see it from the attic, it's been going on a while.

If your roof is getting up in age and you're planning a replacement, ask your contractor what they found on the deck. The answer tells you a lot about how carefully they're working.
