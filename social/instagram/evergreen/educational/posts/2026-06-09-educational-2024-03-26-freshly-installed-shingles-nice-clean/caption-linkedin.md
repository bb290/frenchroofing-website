# Caption (linkedin)

A freshly installed roof should look exactly like this. Clean lines, consistent spacing, every course running true from the ridge to the eave.

For property managers and GCs, that visual uniformity is actually a quality signal worth knowing. Wavy rows, uneven tabs, or lifted edges after the first season usually trace back to inconsistent nailing patterns or a starter strip that wasn't set right. The problems don't always show up as a leak right away, but they tend to surface within a year or two.
