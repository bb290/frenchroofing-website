# Caption (instagram)

Tear-off day is when a roof finally tells the truth.

Once the old shingles come off, we can see the deck underneath. Soft spots, rot, popped fasteners, sheathing that's been holding moisture for years without anyone knowing. It's the part of the job most homeowners never see, but it matters more than almost anything else we do.

A new roof is only as good as what it's sitting on. If we nail new shingles over a compromised deck, you've got a beautiful surface hiding a real problem. So when something's not right, we stop and talk to you before we keep going. New shingles go on after, not around it.

If your roof is coming up on a replacement, ask your contractor what they do when they find deck damage. The answer tells you a lot.
