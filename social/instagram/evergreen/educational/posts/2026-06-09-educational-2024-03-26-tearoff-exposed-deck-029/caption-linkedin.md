# Caption (linkedin)

Tear-off day is when a roof finally tells the truth.

Once the old shingles come off, the deck is exposed, and that's where you find out what the last installation was hiding. Soft spots, rotted sheathing, moisture damage that's been building for years. Most homeowners never see this part of the process, but it's one of the most important calls we make on a job.

A new roof is only as good as the surface it's fastened to. At French Roofing, when we find deck damage during a tear-off, we stop and communicate with the property owner before moving forward. No surprises buried under new shingles.

If you're a property manager or GC evaluating roofing bids, it's worth asking each contractor how they handle deck issues mid-job. The process tells you more about their workmanship than any sales pitch.
