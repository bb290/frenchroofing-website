# Caption (instagram)

Most people don't see the inside of their roof until something's already gone wrong. This is what that looks like.

Exposed rafters, framing, and moldy insulation. The mold tells us water has been getting in for a while, not days, weeks or months at minimum. By the time insulation looks like this, the leak isn't new.

The tricky part is that you can have active moisture damage up here and still have a ceiling that looks perfectly fine downstairs. The water finds the path of least resistance, sometimes that's straight down, sometimes it wanders along framing until it hits something absorbent.

If you're seeing any soft spots on your ceiling, a musty smell in an upstairs room, or shingle granules piling up in your gutters, those are worth paying attention to before the framing gets involved.
