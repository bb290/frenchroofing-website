# Caption (facebook)

Most people don't see the inside of their roof until something's already gone wrong. This is what that looks like.

Exposed rafters, framing, and moldy insulation. The mold tells us water has been getting in for a while, not days, weeks or months at minimum. By the time insulation looks like this, the leak isn't new.

The tricky part is that you can have active moisture damage up here and a ceiling downstairs that looks perfectly fine. Water wanders along framing until it finds something absorbent, so the drip spot and the source are rarely in the same place.

If you're seeing soft spots on your ceiling, a musty smell in an upstairs room, or granules piling up in your gutters, those are worth a look before the framing gets involved.
