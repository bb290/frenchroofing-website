# Caption (instagram)

Mid-install is one of those moments most homeowners never see. The old roof is off, the deck is clean, and the new system is going down course by course. This is where the real work shows.

What you're looking at here is a two-day job in progress. Most full replacements take about two days. Simpler roofs, smaller footprints, good weather, we can sometimes wrap it in one. But we don't rush the timeline to hit a number. We let the job set the pace.

The reason it takes as long as it does is that each layer matters. Underlayment, ice and water shield at the eaves and valleys, starter course, then shingles row by row. Skipping steps to save an hour shows up later, usually in a claim or a callback.

If you've ever wondered why your neighbor's crew was done by noon and ours is still there the next morning, this is often why.
