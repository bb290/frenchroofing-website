# Caption (instagram)

Exposed decking and rafters tell you a lot about a roof before a single shingle goes down.

When we strip a roof to the deck, this is what we're looking at. Any soft spots, rot, or broken sheathing gets fixed before we ever open a bundle of shingles. It's not glamorous work, but skipping it means the new roof is sitting on a bad foundation.

In the Pacific Northwest, moisture works its way into decking slowly. You won't see it from the ground, and sometimes you won't see it until the old roof comes off. We find damaged sheathing on a fair number of jobs in the Damascus and Clackamas area, especially on roofs that went a few years longer than they should have.

If a roofer doesn't mention checking the deck as part of a replacement, that's worth asking about. A solid roof starts below the shingles.
