# Caption (gmb)

Skylight flashing is one of the first things we check on a Damascus or Clackamas area roof inspection.

Debris, leaves, pine needles, and dirt tends to collect right at the flashing perimeter around a skylight. That debris holds moisture against the flashing compound through our long PNW rainy season, and over time it causes the material to erode faster than the rest of the roof. A small gap there can become a steady leak path before you notice anything from inside.

Caught early, it's a straightforward repair. Left alone, it usually finds the ceiling eventually.

If you've got skylights and can't remember the last time someone looked at the flashing, it's worth a quick check, especially heading into fall.
