# Caption (linkedin)

Skylights add a lot to a home, but they're one of the spots we pay closest attention to on any roof inspection.

The flashing around a skylight is a seam where roofing materials meet the skylight frame. Debris collects there fast. Leaves, pine needles, and dirt trap moisture against the flashing compound through months of PNW rain, and that accelerates erosion. What starts as surface wear can open a gap that sends water into the framing before anyone sees a stain on the ceiling.

For property managers and GCs, this is worth building into any routine inspection checklist. It's a small thing to catch early and a more involved fix once water has had a season or two to work.
