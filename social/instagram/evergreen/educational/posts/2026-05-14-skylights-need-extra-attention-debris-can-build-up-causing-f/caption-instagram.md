# Caption (instagram)

Skylights are great until they're not.

The flashing around a skylight works hard. It's a seam in your roof where two different materials meet, and debris loves to collect right there. Leaves, pine needles, dirt, sitting wet through every PNW rainstorm. Over time that moisture speeds up the breakdown of the flashing compound, and a small gap turns into a leak path before you'd ever notice from inside.

We check the flashing perimeter any time we're near a skylight on a job. Most of the time it just needs a good cleaning and a close look. Occasionally we find erosion that's moved past the surface. Caught early, it's a straightforward fix. Ignored, it usually finds the ceiling eventually.

If you've got a skylight, pull up a photo from your phone or just get eyes on it from the ground after a windstorm. Debris sitting against the frame is the thing to watch for.
