# Caption (facebook)

Skylights are great until they're not.

The flashing around a skylight works hard. It's a seam where two different materials meet, and debris loves to collect right there. Leaves, pine needles, and dirt sit wet through every PNW rainstorm, and that moisture speeds up the breakdown of the flashing compound. A small gap turns into a leak path before you'd notice anything from inside.

We check the flashing perimeter any time we're near a skylight on a job. Most of the time it just needs a good cleaning and a close look. Occasionally we find erosion that's moved past the surface. Caught early, it's a straightforward fix. Ignored, it usually finds the ceiling eventually.

If you've got a skylight, keep an eye on debris buildup around the frame, especially after a windstorm or a stretch of heavy rain. That's usually where trouble starts.
