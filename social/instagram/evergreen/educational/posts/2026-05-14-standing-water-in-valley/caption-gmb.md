# Caption (gmb)

Roof valley issues in Damascus and the greater Portland metro area are something we see regularly this time of year.

Valleys are where two roof planes meet and they handle a high volume of water. When leaves, fir needles, and moss debris build up, water slows down and sits instead of draining off. Over time, standing water works under shingle tabs and valley flashing seams, and what started as a simple cleaning becomes a flashing repair.

In the Pacific Northwest, the combination of heavy rainfall and organic debris makes valley maintenance one of the most practical things a homeowner can do to extend the life of their roof.
