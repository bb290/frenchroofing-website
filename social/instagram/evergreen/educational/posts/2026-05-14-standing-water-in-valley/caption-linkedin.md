# Caption (linkedin)

Standing water in a roof valley doesn't announce itself, and by the time it does, the damage is usually already done.

Valleys are the highest-volume drainage points on a roof. When debris accumulates, the water slows, sits, and eventually works its way under shingle tabs and valley flashing seams. In the Pacific Northwest, we've got both the rainfall and the organic debris (fir needles, leaves, moss) to make this a regular problem.

For property managers and GCs with multiple buildings in the Damascus, Clackamas, or Happy Valley area, valley condition is one of the first things we check during a roof assessment. It's a small thing to catch early and a more expensive conversation once the flashing is compromised.
