# Caption (gmb)

Roof maintenance in Damascus, OR, especially with heavy tree cover.

Pine needles pile up fast in roof valleys, along ridgelines, and in gutters. The problem isn't the needles themselves, it's the moisture they trap underneath. That's where moss and algae start, and over time it speeds up granule loss and shortens the life of your shingles.

If your home has trees hanging over the roof, plan on two cleanings a year: once in late spring and once before fall rains arrive. It's the simplest thing you can do to protect what's already up there.
