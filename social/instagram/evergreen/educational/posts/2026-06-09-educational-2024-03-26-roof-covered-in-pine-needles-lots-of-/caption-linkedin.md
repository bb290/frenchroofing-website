# Caption (linkedin)

Pine needles are one of the more overlooked roof maintenance problems in the Pacific Northwest, especially on properties with heavy tree cover.

They pack into valleys and gutters, trap moisture against the shingle surface, and create the exact conditions moss and algae need to take hold. Left unchecked, that moisture accelerates granule loss and shortens the effective life of the roof system. For a property manager or owner carrying multiple buildings, that's an avoidable cost.

The fix isn't complicated. Two cleanings a year, one after spring needle drop and one before the fall rains, keeps debris from building up and gives the roof a chance to shed water the way it was installed to.
